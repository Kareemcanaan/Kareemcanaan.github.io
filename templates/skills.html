Computer Science with an Integrated Foundation Year. Requirements, expectations and benefits.

Computer Science with an Integrated Foundation Year is a one year course created to improve your mathematical and scientific skills to give you an opportunity to start the degree in one of the following subject areas

· Computer Science and Mathematics BSc

· Computer Science and Mathematics with Industrial Experience BSc

· Computer Science BSc

· Computer Science with Industrial Experience BSc

Students are accepted from a wide range of educational backgrounds. The entry requirements vary depending on the Mathematics, Further Mathematics, Physics, and Computer Science subjects being taken at A level. Typically, the University would be looking for:

· Grades ABB where a student has 3 relevant subjects

· Grades AAB where a student has 2 relevant subjects

· Grades AAA where a student has 1 relevant subject

· All applicants to the University (from the UK and Overseas)

are required to show evidence of English Language proficiency.

New skills gained through the Foundation Year

On the Foundation Year you will study Mathematics and Physics. In addition, you will take modules in Academic Skills and ICT (Information and Communications Technology). You will also complete a team project, related to your degree course and supervised by an academic from your future Department.

The integrated foundation route has produced outstanding graduates across all of our science and engineering courses. It will assure your potential for success, adaptability, analytical skills and independent thought. Computer Science with an Integrated Foundation Year gives you the preparation in Mathmetics, Physics, ICT, Team Project, and Academic skills that you need to proceed to degree-level courses in the subject area of Computer Science in the Department of Computer Science.

The Foundation Year helps students learn and develop skills:

Time management

This skill will prove helpful when students will do multiple courses, deadlines, and projects throughout the day and so have to manage their time to complete all the tasks.

Communication

This is a skill that will be developed in the Foundation Year in a lot of discussions and workshops with teachers and students throughout the whole programme.

Teamwork

Working with a team will develop teamwork skills where students will communicate together and assign each member to a certain task to be able to reach the common final goal.

Researching

This is a skill that is needed for doing things such as essays and presentations in the Foundation Year to find relevant information from trusted sources.

Presenting Ideas

This skill comes from events where students present their ideas, opinions, and the results of their research to their team and teachers.

Problem Solving

The Foundation Year will help to develop this skill when a student faces a challenging task and has to solve it through their research.

On successful completion of the Foundation Year you will find that this helps greatly in easing your move into the first year of your chosen degree programme.
